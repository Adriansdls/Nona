Online questionnaire, n=1,210 missing cats globally, self-selected participants. Key findings: (1) Only 61% of missing cats found within 1 year; 34% recovered alive by owner within 7 days; very few found alive after 90 days — establishing a sharp temporal decay curve for recovery probability. (2) Physical search increased probability of finding cat alive (p=0.073, borderline); 75% of cats found within 500m of escape point — establishing a tight spatial prior for displaced animals. (3) Outdoor-access cats traveled much farther: 75th-percentile distance 1,609m vs. 137m for indoor-only cats (p≤0.001) — temperament/access history bifurcates the spatial distribution sharply. (4) Highly curious cats more likely found inside someone else's home vs. other personality types. (5) Cats 13x more likely to be returned by non-shelter means. (6) Physical searching recommended as primary strategy within first 7 days. Methodology note: self-selected online sample, retrospective case series, global respondents (not restricted to one jurisdiction).
