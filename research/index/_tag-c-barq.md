- [[about-the-c-barq-c-barq-canine-behavioral-assessment-research-questionnaire]] — About the C-BARQ - C-BARQ: Canine Behavioral Assessment & Research Questionnaire — Official description of C-BARQ (Canine Behavioral Assessment & Research Questionnaire), developed by Hsu and Serpell (2003), University of Pennsylvania. Now contains evaluations for ~50,000 dogs across 300+ breeds. Primary instrument for standardized canine behavioral phenotyping used in research and rescue contexts. Measures 14 behavioral factors: stranger-directed aggression, owner-directed aggression, dog-directed aggression, dog rivalry, stranger-directed fear, nonsocial fear, dog-directed fear, separation-related behavior, attachment/attention-seeking, trainability, chasing, excitability, touch sensitivity, energy level. Mini C-BARQ (Duffy, Kruger & Serpell, 2015) is designed for shelter intake screening. C-BARQ database includes 35,000+ working assistance dog evaluations. Used in 70+ published studies since 2004. Key application for rescue platforms: mini C-BARQ at intake provides objective behavioral screening superior to breed-based assumptions; predicts training success in guide dog programs; identifies behavioral risks for adoption placement. Individual dog C-BARQ score is compared to population averages from thousands of dogs — this is the correct architecture for individual temperament assessment over breed priors. `review`

							| Dog behavior — 2025 peer-reviewed study (Dog Behavior journal, vol 11 no 1) on behavioral variation across 10 sighthound breeds plus Lurchers, using C-BARQ sections, n=768. Key findings: sighthounds show very low aggression across all breeds; high predatory drive (chasing cats/birds differs by breed, Saluki highest, Piccolo Levriero Italiano lowest, p<0.001); fair attachment/affection (Piccolo Levriero Italiano and Irish Wolfhound show most owner-shadowing and interaction-soliciting, p<0.001). Authors note breed/functional type role in behavior is controversial, with effect sizes ranging from negligible to substantial across the literature.
