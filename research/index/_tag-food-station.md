Defines 'chasing' broadly: any forward movement toward the dog, including cars, ATVs/quads (motor sound triggers flight before visual contact), search parties on foot. Dog in fight-or-flight does not distinguish owner from predator — 'you are a PREDATOR at that moment.' Key lure sequence: get low/flat on ground immediately; avoid eye contact; speak softly/calmly; allow dog to approach on its own terms; do not reach — let dog touch first, multiple times if skittish. - [[stay-out-of-the-woods-missing-animal-response-network]] — Stay Out of the Woods - Missing Animal Response Network — MAR Network explanation of why wooded areas require minimal-intrusion protocols. Lost dogs gravitate to woods/cover for safety from perceived threats. Search parties, ATVs, drones, and even foot-traffic on dead leaves/branches can push a dog out of its established shelter and into fatal territory. Cortisol flooding the brain causes sympathetic nervous system to temporarily block memory — dog doesn't recognize owner/guardian until parasympathetic system reengages via calming. Case study: dog pushed out of woods by well-meaning search party, traveled opposite direction, hit by train. Case study (Borzoi 'Princess'): human interference at every sighting caused her to flee from Wyoming to Colorado and back repeatedly until she was hit on highway 34 in Colorado. Protocol: keep area quiet; set food station (smelly canned food) and trail camera 10-20 feet into woods during midday (dogs hunker down midday per Alan Beck 'Ecology of the Stray Dog'). Scent-elevation tip: spray food scent high on tree limbs to carry further. Grill food near home if woods are within a few hundred feet — creates scent cloud. Never set trap until confirmed dog is eating at station on trail camera. `review`

Core rule: never set a trap until the dog has been confirmed eating at a specific food station on trail camera. Rationale: dog won't travel long distances to reach a trap, supplies are costly, and a triggered trap at wrong location wastes time. Not all dogs need trapping — gregarious dogs can be lured without traps. Trapping sequence: (1) establish food station with familiar scent article near escape point; (2) confirm dog eating on trail camera; (3) obtain landowner permission if off-property; (4) set humane trap monitored every 1-3 hours (or via cellular game camera sending real-time photos). Trap must be monitored — animal should not be inside trap more than 30 minutes. Overnight: refresh food and tie trap door open (bungee cord) so dog can enter, eat, and build comfort with the trap. Never open trap in field to remove captured dog — take whole trap to enclosed space (garage) first, to prevent escape.
