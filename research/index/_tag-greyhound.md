Documents greyhound-specific stress responses using 4F framework (Fidget/Freeze/Flight/Fight). Key lost-dog insights: (1) FREEZE response is characteristic in greyhounds — 'frozen or stiff posture, pupils dilated, trembling, tense closed mouth, ears back, tail tucked, body hunched, all feet stuck to floor, leaning backwards' — this explains why a lost greyhound may be visible but unresponsive. (2) FLIGHT response details: 'ears back, tail tucked, actively trying to escape — slinking away or running' — greyhounds in flight will not respond to direct approach. (3) Most greyhounds are under-socialized to urban environments (grown up rural, no exposure to traffic, stairs, glass doors, different dogs/people). (4) Tactical advice: approach from oblique angle not direct, crouch/sit, avoid eye contact, turn body away. Food often ineffective when dog is in fear state. (5) Muzzle and martingale collar mandatory off-property — collar slippage is a key escape vector. `evergreen`
