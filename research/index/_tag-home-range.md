A pilot study exploring the movement ecology of farm dogs — Chopra, Enticott & Codling 2024 (Frontiers Vet Sci) — GPS + accelerometer pilot study of 3 free-roaming farm dogs (UK). Core home range 0.54–0.89 km², full range 2.72–3.38 km². Dogs spent 64-74% of time on slow movements (<50 m/min), 11-16% on fast movements (≥100 m/min). Average movement 48-51 m/min across dogs. For 75% of dogs: 7 days sufficient for stable core HR size (10% daily change threshold); 26 days for extended HR. Some dogs needed only 1 day, others up to 53 days. Significant individual variation. - [[what-influences-the-home-range-size-of-free-roaming-domestic-dogs-epidemiology-i]] — What influences the home range size of free-roaming domestic dogs? | Epidemiology & Infection | Cambridge Core — Dürr et al. 2017 (Epidemiology & Infection). GPS study of 135 free-roaming dogs in 8 Aboriginal communities, northern Australia, 215 observations. Key findings: core HR (50% BRB kernel) ranged 0.17-2.33 ha (median 0.35 ha); extended HR (95% BRB kernel) ranged 0.86-40.46 ha (median 4.48 ha). Non-castrated males had the largest home ranges; neutered females second largest. Extended HR was significantly larger in the PRE-wet (dry) season than in the POST-wet (wet) season — because standing water during wet season creates physical barriers to movement, and post-rain food abundance reduces need to travel. Most dogs spent most time around owner's house; majority did not roam further than 250 m diameter; a small subset roamed more extensively. Age and breed not significant predictors. Dog density not significant. This is the largest study of its type and provides the key empirical anchor for dry-season expanded range behavior. `evergreen`
