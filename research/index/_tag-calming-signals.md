Core mechanism: when a dog is in fight-or-flight mode, calling its name by strangers becomes a conditioned trigger for bolting — other people have already called/chased the dog before the owner arrives, so 'calling' is now a fear trigger even for the owner's own voice. Key: olfactory processing shuts down during peak adrenaline state, so even food lures may temporarily fail. Cortisol flooding the brain causes temporary memory loss — owner's face/voice not recognized until calm is restored. Recommended approach: sit/lie flat on ground, avoid direct eye contact, use sideways hip-facing posture (not predator straight-on approach), make lip-smacking 'nummy' sounds, drop food on ground, use crinkly chip bags (dogs associate sound with food), SING (unique sound not used by other failed capturers). Do not whistle, clap, or slap leg — these are triggers used by previous failed capture attempts. Once parasympathetic nervous system re-engages (calming signals activate it), cortisol drops, dog may recognize owner by scent. During full fight-or-flight, olfactory section of brain shuts down — food lures fail at peak panic. `review`
