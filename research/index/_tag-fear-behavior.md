Fear signals: freezing/stillness, raised hackles, head lowered or raised while looking away, staring (defensive dogs), growling, lip wrinkling, snarling. Approach protocol: move toward dog's side (not head-on), avoid direct eye contact, make body 'smaller' by lowering shoulder and angling away, bend down beside dog, keep hands to self, give dog seconds to sniff voluntarily — do not force hand. For leashing: slowly loop lead over head rather than grabbing collar; once leashed, move away and wait for dog to follow — movement itself reduces 'cornered' sensation. If dog panics on leash, release pressure and wait. Walk can take 10 minutes to 1 hour. Goal is gradual habituation to human presence, not forced capture.
