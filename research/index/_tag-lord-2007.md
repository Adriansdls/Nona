Primary outcome: 14% of dogs and 15% of cats became lost at least once in a five-year window. Recovery rate: 93% (95% CI 86–97%) for dogs vs. 75% (95% CI 64–85%) for cats. Of lost dogs, primary recovery method was neighborhood search (49%), then self-return (20%), then ID tag contact (15%). Dogs most commonly lost more than once. No demographic (sex, neuter status, owner education, income, gender) was independently associated with getting lost, but neutered pets and pets of higher-education owners were significantly more likely to be recovered. Shelter search was used by only 6 of 101 successfully recovered dogs; confirms majority of reunions happen outside the shelter system. Limitations: lower-than-expected pet-loss rate, land-line only sample, small subsample of unfound pets, social-desirability bias for ID reporting. `evergreen`
