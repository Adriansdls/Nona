# Orchestrator Notes

## Emerging patterns from search results

### Terrain/movement
- Kat Albrecht's 3-category framework (Gregarious/Aloof/Xenophobic) is the canonical lost pet behavior taxonomy — directly drives corridor model (gregarious = stays near road/people; xenophobic = travels far, avoids all)
- Lost dogs strongly follow "path of least resistance" — corroborates corridor model
- Movement ecology literature (PMC studies on farm dogs, free-roaming dogs in Malawi) provides GPS data on actual ranging behavior
- 70% found within 1 mile; 42% within 400 feet — but this is likely heavily biased by gregarious dogs; xenophobic travelers probably 5-30km range
- The IAABC article may contain research gaps critique useful for adversarial framing

### Scent/wind
- Detector Dogs and Scent Movement (Osterkamp) is the canonical book — need to extract key parameters
- Scent cone ≠ actual cone — turbulence creates patchy distribution
- Thermal inversion at night traps scent near ground — favorable for tracking dogs at night
- SAR handbook from sarbc.org likely has technical parameters

### Water
- 72h without water general limit; drops to 36-48h in extreme heat (35°C+)
- Daily requirement: ~1 oz/lb body weight (~50-60ml/kg)
- No strong evidence on specific water-seeking radius found yet — may be a gap

### Vehicle transport
- ASPCA survey + frequency study (PMC4494319) key for statistics
- Mixed breeds travel farther than purebreds before being picked up — interesting signal about pickup selectivity
- Most lost dogs found by neighborhood search (49%), only 6% at shelter — suggests good samaritan transport is common but they don't go to shelters

### Heat/activity
- Crepuscular activity is strongly documented in dogs
- Temperature threshold: panting begins above 32°C ambient; heat stress risk above ~38°C ambient
- Frontiers 2025 paper on heat stress morphological risk — likely has good data on specific thresholds

### Algarve specific
- Nortada: NNW direction, 15-28 km/h typical, June-September, daily peak in afternoons
- This means afternoon scent disperses north→south; mornings/evenings calmer = scent pools in valleys and low areas
- Algarve summer = very dry → scent persistence degraded; winter wet → scent persists longer
- No specific Algarve/Portugal lost dog data found — gap to flag

## Hypothesis forming
The report should argue that the concentric-radius model fails because it ignores 3 empirically distinct motion regimes: (1) xenophobic dogs that travel far along corridors away from roads/people, (2) gregarious dogs that get transported quickly by good samaritans to new locations, and (3) all dogs after 48-72h that reorganize their ranging around water sources. Each regime needs different intervention logic.

## Coverage gaps to flag
- Water-seeking radius/convergence: no specific studies found yet
- Algarve-specific tourist pickup data: likely doesn't exist formally
- Breed-specific terrain behavior beyond general categories: thin
