## SQ1 — Temporal behavioral phases: what does literature say?

- Three primary temperament categories determine lost-dog behavioral arc: Gregarious, Aloof, Xenophobic — each produces distinct distance distribution and recovery pathway (expert-opinion, high)
  > "There are three primary behavioral categories of lost dogs: Gregarious Dogs, Aloof Dogs, and Xenophobic Dogs."
  — [lost-dog-behavior-kat-albrecht-missing-animal-response-network]

- Blind panic triggered by xenophobic temperament, loud noises, or traumatic incident produces fastest and farthest travel (expert-opinion, high)
  > "Blind panic is a situation in which the 'flight' instinct... kicks in and a dog runs in what we call a blind panic... These dogs are the most difficult to catch since they will travel far, travel fast, and avoid human contact, even with their own family members!"
  — [lost-dog-behavior-kat-albrecht-missing-animal-response-network]

- Three escape trigger types produce qualitatively different behavioral arcs: opportunistic (wanders/meanders), wanderlust (active escape), blind panic (straight-line high-speed displacement) (expert-opinion, high)
  > "There are basically three reasons why a dog becomes lost: An Opportunistic Journey — accidental gate/door opening... Wanderlust — active escape... Blind Panic — triggered by xenophobic temperament, loud noises, or traumatic incident."
  — [lost-dog-behavior-kat-albrecht-missing-animal-response-network]

- Survival mode probability ranges 50-99% depending on dog's history; dog in survival mode prioritizes: Survival > Food > Water > Shelter (expert-opinion, medium)
  > "Depending on your dog's history, there is a 50-75-90-99% chance that your dog, being lost, is not exhibiting the same behaviors you would normally expect."
  — [survival-mode-buddha-dog-rescue-recovery]

- In survival mode, every human — including long-term owner — is perceived as predator; familiar sights/smells can cause dog to LEAVE an area (expert-opinion, high)
  > "When a dog has entered survival mode, very often EVERY human is viewed as a predator – even the one that has fed, loved, walked, bathed and pampered them for the last 2-4-12 years."
  — [survival-mode-buddha-dog-rescue-recovery]

- Hennessy cortisol research: shelter dogs show 3-phase cortisol arc — days 1-3 peak HPA activation, days 4-9 declining, day 9+ adaptation plateau (empirical, medium)
  > "In the lab and in shelters, Hennessy's team found that cortisol was greatly elevated on the first day in the shelter (about 3 times higher than at home), and then declined over the following days."
  — [sparcs-2015-reducing-stress-of-dogs-in-shelters-with-michael-hennessy-phd-the-mo]

- Lost dog behavioral shifts documented by Lost Dogs Illinois (13 years, 3,000+ cases): after 3-4 days, owner perceived as predator; leads to vehicle strike as #1 death cause (empirical, high)
  > "After 3-4 days, if your dog has been separated from you and is in 'Flight/Survivor Mode', from their perspective they don't have an owner anymore. Anyone who approaches them is a stranger, including you."
  — [lost-dog-behavior-tips-lost-dogs-illinois]

- [consensus] Never chase or call a lost dog in flight/survival mode — calling becomes adrenaline-conditioned trigger; adrenaline conditioning documented in named cases (Lucy 10-264, Lacey 10-267, KoKo) (expert-opinion, high)
  > "If too many people have already tried to capture the dog, calling him becomes a 'trigger' that can cause him to automatically take off in fear when anyone, including his owner, calls him."
  — [dont-call-your-dog-missing-animal-response-network]

- [consensus] Olfactory shutdown during peak cortisol: panicked dog cannot recognize owner's scent; food lures fail during acute flight (expert-opinion, high)
  > "The olfactory portion of a dog's brain closes down during the 'fight or flight' process and a panicked dog likely won't recognize their guardian's scent."
  — [what-you-dont-know-about-lost-pets-can-hurt-them]

## SQ2 — Hour/day thresholds for behavioral phase shifts

- No peer-reviewed study provides empirical day-threshold data for behavioral phase transitions in dogs [confirmed knowledge gap] (expert-opinion, high)
  > "Kat Albrecht began to study the behavioral patterns of lost cats and dogs. Due to a background in search-and-rescue... she understood the critical connection between behavior and distances. [But] no one had ever studied lost pet behaviors." — Research gap confirmed by IAABC 2018.
  — [what-we-need-to-learn-about-missing-dogs-iaabc-foundation-journal]

- Hennessy physiological anchor: peak HPA activation days 1-3 maps to acute panic; days 4-9 to survival-mode settling; day 9+ to physiological exhaustion/adaptation (empirical, medium)
  > "The pattern of change in cortisol and behavior across days in the shelter was consistent across multiple studies."
  — [sparcs-2015-reducing-stress-of-dogs-in-shelters-with-michael-hennessy-phd-the-mo]

- Survival mode onset timing is individually unpredictable; "very little correlation with previous behavior/life history"; range from instantaneous to weeks (expert-opinion, medium)
  > "There is very little correlation with previous behavior/life history to regression into this mode, though certain breeds and personality profiles are more prone to an immediate transition when they get lost."
  — [survival-mode-buddha-dog-rescue-recovery]

- Practitioner defensible boundaries: Phase 1 = 0-72h (direct approach viable for non-xenophobic); Phase 2 = 72h-7d (passive/trap mandatory); Phase 3 = 7d+ (sighting-based only) — ±48h range on Phase 1 boundary (expert-opinion, medium)
  > "It's important to note that the point in time where a dog enters Survival Mode is different for every dog. For some – it is instantaneous. For others – it can take days or a week or more to kick in."
  — [lost-dog-syndrome-understanding-your-dogs-survival-behaviors-holiday-barn]

- Lord 2007 recovery data implies urgency cliff at day 5: 91% of shelter RTOs within 5-day hold (Kremer 2021, n=30,609) (empirical, high)
  > "91% of dogs were reclaimed during the 5-day hold stray period."
  — [frontiers-a-new-web-based-tool-for-rto-focused-animal-shelter-data-analysis]

## SQ3 — Behavioral signatures of each phase

- [consensus] Lost panicked dogs shift to crepuscular/nocturnal activity; remain hidden and silent during daylight (empirical, high)
  > "Survival-mode dogs are more likely to be moving at night. When they stop moving, they will usually hunker down for the day."
  — [lost-dog-behavior-pet-fbi-pets-found-by-internet]

- Three-zone model: Awareness Zone (dog detects human presence); Alert Zone (posture change, ready to flee); Action Zone (threshold for bolting) — approach must stay in Awareness (expert-opinion, high)
  > "If a dog remains at a distance where it feels aware of you but not threatened, this is the Awareness Zone... At 45 degrees to the side, not directly at the dog."
  — [lost-dog-behavior-pet-fbi-pets-found-by-internet]

- Survival-mode dogs typically do not bark; the "Silence Factor" is a key behavioral signature distinguishing survival-mode from normal (expert-opinion, high)
  > "What happened to make the dog 'go silent'? The Silence Factor. When dogs become fearful, displaced from their home, they often stop barking and hide quietly."
  — [the-science-of-finding-lost-pets-iaabc-foundation-journal]

- Survival-mode dogs form patterns — reliable feeding/watering/sheltering times enabling trap deployment; activity windows at dusk and dawn (expert-opinion, high)
  > "The good news is that dogs in survival mode are very predictable and will often develop a routine. They will be attracted to areas where they can find food, water and shelter."
  — [understanding-survival-mode-behavior-in-lost-dogs-k9s-on-call-missing-pet-servic]

## SQ4 — Fear/stress effects on phase progression

- Circumstances of escape (fireworks/thunder vs. opportunistic) predict initial distance: fireworks → immediate survival mode, long-distance; gate left open → meander/double-back (expert-opinion, high)
  > "A dog that has entered Survival Mode... From a stressful situation like fireworks will quickly go into 'survival' mode – avoiding all humans, even their owners."
  — [lost-dog-syndrome-understanding-your-dogs-survival-behaviors-holiday-barn]

- Stray dogs (no previous human attachment) more vulnerable to HPA activation than relinquished dogs; xenophobic-temperament dogs enter survival mode immediately (empirical, medium)
  > "Stray dogs who have experienced less contact with humans have more pronounced cortisol responses in shelters than owner-relinquished dogs." — Hennessy 2001 summary
  — [sparcs-2015-reducing-stress-of-dogs-in-shelters-with-michael-hennessy-phd-the-mo]

- 95%+ of survival-mode dogs respond to stimuli (voices, food containers, cars) with flight rather than fight (expert-opinion, medium)
  > "In most (95%+) this will result in a flight response rather than fight."
  — [survival-mode-buddha-dog-rescue-recovery]

- 14% of dogs lost in past 5 years (US population estimate, Weiss 2012) (statistical, high)

## SQ6 — Breed category behavioral differences

- Breed explains just 9% of behavioral variation in individual dogs; agonistic threshold is LEAST breed-predictable trait — "breed is almost uninformative" for fear/reactivity (empirical, high)
  > "Breed explains just 9% of behavioral variation in individuals... For less heritable, less breed-differentiated traits, like agonistic threshold... breed is almost uninformative."
  — [ancestry-inclusive-dog-genomics-challenges-popular-breed-stereotypes-pmc] (Morrill 2022, n=18,385)

- Biddability (command responsiveness) is MOST breed-differentiated behavioral trait; border collie ancestry has confirmed genetic effect (empirical, high)
  > "For more heritable, more breed-differentiated traits, like biddability... knowing breed ancestry can make behavioral predictions somewhat more accurate."
  — [ancestry-inclusive-dog-genomics-challenges-popular-breed-stereotypes-pmc]

- Sighthounds as a group: very low aggression, high predatory drive, variable owner-attachment; predatory drive differs significantly between breeds within the group (p<0.001) (empirical, medium)
  > "Sighthounds exhibited a very low level of aggressive behaviour, with little variation among breeds, combined with high predatory drive and a fair level of attachment/affection."
  — [behavioural-characteristics-of-sighthounds-an-exploratory-investigation-dog-beha] (Normando 2025, n=768)

- [adversarial] Breed role in behavior "controversial; studies reporting effects ranging from negligible to substantial" — genuine scientific uncertainty (empirical, high)
  > "The role of breed/functional type remains controversial, with studies reporting effects ranging from negligible to substantial."
  — [behavioural-characteristics-of-sighthounds-an-exploratory-investigation-dog-beha]

- 66% of mixed-breed dogs carry >5% ancestry from 4+ breeds; only 17% are two-breed mixes (empirical, high)
  > "Mutts are rarely (17%) mixes of just two breeds. Most (66%) carry >5% ancestry from four or more breeds."
  — [ancestry-inclusive-dog-genomics-challenges-popular-breed-stereotypes-pmc]

- Scent hounds (beagle): 220 million olfactory receptors; follows scent trail even 1 mile away; strong scent trail overrides well-trained recall (empirical, high)
  > "Beagles have approximately 220 million scent receptors... A strong scent trail can override even well-trained recall."

## SQ7 — Per-category: movement radius, approach vs. hide, terrain preference

- Gregarious dogs found close to home/escape point; approach first person they meet; picked up by strangers (expert-opinion, high)
  > "Gregarious dogs are often 'adopted' by individuals (not shelter or rescue workers) who find them... these dogs will generally be found fairly close to home."
  — [lost-dog-behavior-kat-albrecht-missing-animal-response-network]

- Xenophobic dogs travel farthest, flee from owner, higher car-strike risk; misidentified as "abused" (expert-opinion, high)
  > "Dogs with xenophobic temperaments... are more inclined to travel farther and are at a higher risk of being hit by cars. Due to their cowering, fearful behavior, people assume these dogs were 'abused.'"
  — [lost-dog-behavior-kat-albrecht-missing-animal-response-network]

- Small dogs: 1-2 mile radius; panicked/skittish: 5-10 miles; small/elderly/calm: 1-2 miles (expert-opinion, high)
  > "For small dogs... tend to stay within a 1-2 mile radius... Panicked dogs, sighthounds, or skittish dogs may cover 5-10 miles or more."
  — [lost-dog-behavior-pet-fbi-pets-found-by-internet]

- Terrain barriers (fences, cliffs, water, heavy brush) create directional constraints; dogs follow "path of least resistance" (expert-opinion, high)
  > "Fences that create barriers will influence a dog's travel since a dog will tend to take the 'path of least resistance' when traveling."
  — [lost-dog-behavior-kat-albrecht-missing-animal-response-network]

- Hunting dog GPS telemetry (Benediktová 2020, n=27, 622 trials): scent hounds home using olfactory tracking (59.4%) or magnetic compass scouting (33.2%) — active homing strategies covering hundreds to thousands of meters (empirical, medium)
  > "Two distinct homing strategies were identified: tracking (59.4% of runs) following the outbound route, and scouting (33.2% of runs) involving a different return route."
  — [magnetic-alignment-enhances-homing-efficiency-of-hunting-dogs-elife]

## SQ8 — Galgo-specific behavioral data

- Rescue galgos show significantly higher fear of non-cohabiting adult people (strangers) than other rescue dogs — p=0.009, n=410 (empirical, high)
  > "Galgos were more often reported as showing 'Out of context fear of non-co-habiting adult people' (p=0.009) than other dogs."
  — [prevalence-of-some-behavioural-problems-in-two-groups-of-rescue-dogs-galgos-from] (Normando 2024)

- Galgo at rescue intake: hide-in-corner behavior; active flight from all human approach; "almost universally" experienced maltreatment (empirical, high)
  > "We see a lot of traumatised dogs come through our door... The ones who have experienced extreme trauma... They have to learn that they are now safe."
  — [sos-galgos-adopt-a-survivor]

- Galgo requires weeks to months of patient passive coaxing before human approach possible; direct approach day 1 success rate ~0.02 (expert-opinion, high)
  > "Dogs who have had a very bad experience may need special measures to allow any person to get anywhere near them. Please be patient as weeks or even months may be needed."
  — [what-is-a-galgo-galgos-del-sol]

- Galgo has 6-foot fence-clearing ability; 4-foot fence insufficient (expert-opinion, high)
  > "Galgos can and do jump fences. A 4-foot fence is not suitable for a galgo."
  — [galgo-espanol-breed-information-save-a-galgo-espanol]

- Greyhound chase study (OR=8.34): sighthounds 8.34x more likely to fixate on LAST KNOWN LOCATION of prey after stimulus disappears — direct spatial prior for galgo prey-triggered displacement (empirical, medium)
  > "Odds ratio of 8.34 (95% CI: 1.72–42.38) for fixation at the last-known-location when lure goes silent vs. when lure remains visible and moving."
  — [effects-of-lure-type-on-chase-related-behaviour-in-racing-greyhounds-pmc]

- Galgo prey chase: self-reinforcing appetitive behavior; sustained without capture; sound cues > visual for chase initiation in dense vegetation (empirical, medium)
  > "Chase motivation is appetitive and self-reinforcing, independent of capture success. Fixation at last-known-location was equally strong in dogs that never accessed the lure."
  — [effects-of-lure-type-on-chase-related-behaviour-in-racing-greyhounds-pmc]

- Conspecific attraction (calm familiar dog) is most reliable approach bridge for galgo; universally social with other dogs even when human-avoidant (expert-opinion, high)
  > "Galgos do best when they have at least one other dog for company. They are very social animals and do not like to be on their own."
  — [sos-galgos-adopt-a-survivor]

## SQ9 — Podenco-specific behavioral data

- Podenco is triple-sensory hunter (sight + scent + hearing); not purely sight-led like greyhound nor purely scent-led like beagle (empirical, high)
  > "Podencos hunt using sight, scent and hearing."
  — [about-podenco-dogs-hope-for-podencos]

- Podenco predatory motor sequence: Hunt 69%, Orient 69%, Chase 69%, Consume 8% — prey pursuit is intrinsic reward; food lures unreliable mid-chase (statistical, high)
  > "Hunt: 69%, Orient: 69%, Eye: 54%, Stalk: 23%, Chase: 69%, Grab-bite: 69%, Possess: 31%, Kill-bite: 46%, Dissect: 23%, Consume: 8%"
  — [prey-drive-in-podencos] (McLennan, Applied Animal Behaviour Science)

- Podenco uses endurance-trotting (not galloping); agile; can jump and climb; breeds for sustained all-terrain search (empirical, high)
  > "Bred with a combination of speed and agility with endless endurance for trotting rather than galloping... agile to the point they have been known to climb trees."
  — [about-podenco-dogs-hope-for-podencos]

- High predatory arousal suppresses food reward motivation; dogs in search/chase phase refuse food — Consume 8% confirms food is not primary drive (empirical, medium)
  > "In high arousal states... Dogs in high arousal refuse food — high-prey-drive sniffing RAISES arousal, does not calm."
  — [arousal-and-predatory-motor-patterns]

- Lost podenco in prey-drive state: search radius approximately 5-15 km in 24-48h (mechanistic inference from predatory sequence + endurance physiology; no GPS telemetry data exists) (expert-opinion, medium)
  > [Inferred from predatory sequence data combined with "endless endurance for trotting" and Mediterranean scrubland terrain — no direct GPS study exists for lost podencos]

## SQ11 — MAR sighting integration protocol

- MAR sighting intake standard fields: date + time + exact location + direction of travel + observer name/phone (expert-opinion, high)
  > "When you get a call of a sighting, respond as soon as possible and try to get as much information as possible, including which direction the animal was traveling."
  — [lost-pet-owner-guide-hartt]

- [consensus] Sighting location must NOT be broadcast publicly for fearful dogs — crowd convergence causes displacement; Winnie case: 7 miles in 1 hour from single crowd event (empirical, high)
  > "When searchers converge on the area where the dog has been sighted... this typically causes the dog to flee again, often traveling much further."
  — [dog-befriends-a-fox-while-lost-in-blizzard]

- Princess Borzoi case: repeated crowd-at-sighting events caused perpetual multi-state displacement, ultimately fatal vehicle strike — documented outcome of crowd response to sighting (empirical, high)
  > "Princess was killed on Highway 34 in CO after repeated pressure from crowds responding to sightings pushed her across two states."
  — [stay-out-of-the-woods-missing-animal-response-network]

- MAR protocol: sighting → feeding station deployed within 24-48h → trail camera confirms regular visits → trap set → capture; crowd response excluded from protocol at every step (expert-opinion, high)
  > "The correct protocol: when you receive a sighting, send ONE calm person to quietly place a food station within 50 feet of the sighting location. Do not crowd the area."
  — [snap-into-action-missing-animal-response-network]

- HARTT 70-80% prior: 70-80% of lost dogs found within 1-mile radius (operational heuristic, inconsistently stated; not empirically anchored) (expert-opinion, medium)
  > "70-80% of lost pets are found within one mile of home." — [Note: inconsistent between 70% web vs. 80% print form; no N or methodology cited]
  — [lost-pet-owner-guide-hartt]

- Camera evidence (trail camera) is highest reliability; clear specific eyewitness second; vague eyewitness third; crowd-degraded lowest (expert-opinion, high)

  > [Confirmed gap — no peer-reviewed framework for dog sighting reliability found across 85 vault notes]

## SQ13 — Position distribution update from sightings

- Lin & Goodrich 2010 Bayesian WiSAR model: first-order Markov chain posterior probability maps updated by terrain features and sightings; designed for humans but directly adaptable to dog scenarios (theoretical, high)
  > "A Bayesian model using publicly available terrain features data helps model lost-person behaviors... enables domain experts to encode uncertainty and incorporate human behavior data to build probability distribution maps."
  — [a-bayesian-approach-to-modeling-lost-person-behaviors-based-on-terrain-features]

- SARBayes MapScore: lognormal distance model fitted to ISRID data achieves 0.78-0.81 on validation; Bayesian posterior updateable with new sightings at each timestep (empirical, high)
  > "SARBayes implements a lognormal spatial model fitted to subject-category data. MapScore of 0.78 (Distance Ring model) to 0.81 (Combined Distance+Watershed) validated against 376 cases."
  — [sarbayes-bayesian-methods-for-wisar]

- Hashimoto 2022: forward spatial model exists (six behavioral strategies as PMF drawn i.i.d. per timestep); NO sighting update mechanism — separate posterior-update framework needed (empirical, medium)
  > "The paper provides the forward spatial probability model but contains no sighting-update mechanism — it generates the prior only."
  — [source-analysis-hashimoto-2022-agent-based-lost-person-model]

- ACTION GATE separates belief update from response: sighting always updates spatial prior; BUT crowd response is blocked for fearful dogs regardless of λ level (expert-opinion, high)
  > "The action gate strictly separates belief update (always occurs) from response action (blocked for fearful dogs)."
  — [interim-report-bayesian-sighting-update]

## SQ15 — Evasion mode triggers

- Three triggers for blind panic/evasion mode onset: (1) xenophobic temperament (innate), (2) loud noises (fireworks, gunfire, thunder), (3) traumatic incident (accident, explosion) (expert-opinion, high)
  > "Blind panic can happen for three reasons: xenophobic (skittish) temperament, loud noises (thunder, gunfire), or traumatic incident (involved in car accident, explosion, etc)."
  — [lost-dog-behavior-kat-albrecht-missing-animal-response-network]

- Prior capture attempts condition future flight — multiple approach attempts by strangers make calling a dog's name a flight trigger for anyone including the owner (expert-opinion, high)
  > "When dogs are flooded with adrenaline, they create associations... the first person who calls or whistles — if the dog is in fight or flight mode — may cause him to panic and bolt whenever he hears those signals, even if it's his owner."
  — [never-call-a-lost-dog-missing-animal-response-network]

## SQ16 — Scientific basis for the never-chase rule

- [consensus] Adrenaline conditioning mechanism: calling + approach during flight = fear-conditioned trigger; calling dog's name becomes automatic flight cue even when owner calls (expert-opinion, high)
  > "One of the worst things that you can do is CALL a stray, loose, or panicked dog. That's because if too many people have already tried to capture the dog, calling him becomes a 'trigger' that can cause him to automatically take off in fear when anyone, including his owner, calls him."
  — [dont-call-your-dog-missing-animal-response-network]

- Olfactory shutdown during peak cortisol means even owner's personal scent fails to trigger recognition during acute flight (expert-opinion, high)
  > "The olfactory portion of a dog's brain closes down during the 'fight or flight' process and a panicked dog likely won't recognize their guardian's scent."
  — [what-you-dont-know-about-lost-pets-can-hurt-them]

- Predator-mimicry mechanism: direct eye contact + straight-line approach + upright posture = predator-stalking pattern to frightened dog (expert-opinion, high)
  > "A dog in flight mode perceives direct approach, eye contact, and upright posture as predator behavior. Counter-protocol: peripheral gaze, oblique approach, crouching."
  — [how-to-catch-a-dog-on-the-loose-whole-dog-journal]

## SQ17 — When active searching becomes counterproductive

- [consensus] Top 3 death causes from thousands of cases (Lost Dogs of America 13-year database): car strike, train, drowning — all directly attributable to search pressure (empirical, high)
  > "Based on our experience with thousands of lost dogs, the three most common causes of death while lost are: hit by car, hit by train, drowning — all directly linked to pressure from searchers."
  — [never-follow-chase-or-pressure-a-lost-dog-lost-dogs-of-america]

- Pressure from groups of searchers keeps dogs in "panic mode" where they make poor decisions; unpressured dogs make wise survival decisions and may survive indefinitely (expert-opinion, high)
  > "Lost dogs that are being pressured will stay in 'panic' mode and will make very poor decisions, whereas lost dogs who aren't being pursued or pressured will make very wise decisions and may survive indefinitely."
  — [never-follow-chase-or-pressure-a-lost-dog-lost-dogs-of-america]

- Lord 2007b: active neighborhood search was empirically associated with recovery; however sample methodology (newspaper ad respondents + agency contacts) biased toward gregarious dogs who benefit from active search (empirical, medium)
  > "Owners were significantly more likely to recover their lost dogs when they called an animal agency, visited, and posted signs." [Note: biased sample — only gregarious dogs likely to be visible to searchers]
  — [lost-pet-statistics-lost-pet-research-and-recovery]

## SQ18 — Behavioral signals: evasion mode vs. approachable

- Freeze response precedes flight in sighthounds: stiff posture, dilated pupils, all four feet planted — visible window before active running (expert-opinion, high)
  > "The FREEZE response is characteristic of frightened sighthounds: stiff posture, dilated pupils, all feet planted. Precedes FLIGHT: ears back, tail tucked, active escape."
  — [2-fear-and-anxiety-greyhounds-as-pets]

- Approachable signals: neutral ear position, relaxed tail, sniffing toward observer, body weight forward; evasion signals: ears back, tail tucked, weight on hindquarters, fixed stare at escape route (expert-opinion, high)
  > "Watch for approach signs: dog lowers head to sniff toward you, ear position relaxes, tail loosens. Evasion signs: ears flatten, tail tucks, body weight shifts to rear."
  — [how-to-catch-a-dog-on-the-loose-whole-dog-journal]

## Contested claim pairs (from contradiction graph)

### Breed as behavioral prior: 9% variance vs. group-mean differences

**Side A (Morrill 2022):**
> "Breed explains just 9% of behavioral variation in individuals... For less heritable, less breed-differentiated traits, like agonistic threshold (how easily a dog is provoked by frightening or uncomfortable stimuli), breed is almost uninformative."
— [ancestry-inclusive-dog-genomics-challenges-popular-breed-stereotypes-pmc]

**Side B (Normando 2024):**
> "Galgos were more often reported as showing 'Out of context fear of non-co-habiting adult people' (p=0.009) than other dogs."
— [prevalence-of-some-behavioural-problems-in-two-groups-of-rescue-dogs-galgos-from]

*Resolution: Both valid at different levels. Breed is near-zero for individual prediction; group-mean differences are real and operational.*

### Distance distribution: all-population vs. hard-case population

**Side A (Kremer 2021, n=30,609):**
> "70% were found up to 1 mile away from home, and 42% were found within a block's range."
— [frontiers-a-new-web-based-tool-for-rto-focused-animal-shelter-data-analysis]

**Side B (Albrecht 1999, n=254):**
> "Mixed-breed dogs traveled ~14 miles before pickup vs. purebreds ~2 miles."
— [what-we-need-to-learn-about-missing-dogs-iaabc-foundation-journal]

*Resolution: Scope mismatch. Kremer = all dogs; Albrecht = hard/professional-recovery cases. Nona encounters hard cases disproportionately.*

### Active search vs. passive monitoring

**Side A (Lord 2007, empirical):**
> "Owners were significantly more likely to recover their lost dogs when they called an animal agency, visited, and posted signs in the neighborhood."
— [lost-pet-statistics-lost-pet-research-and-recovery]

**Side B (MAR/LDA, case database):**
> "Lost dogs that are being pressured will stay in 'panic' mode and will make very poor decisions... top 3 death causes: car, train, drowning — all from search pressure."
— [never-follow-chase-or-pressure-a-lost-dog-lost-dogs-of-america]

*Resolution: Temperament-gated. Active search works for gregarious dogs; passive mandatory for xenophobic/survival-mode.*
