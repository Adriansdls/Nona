## Genuine knowledge gaps (not fetching failures)

1. **No empirical phase-threshold data for dogs** — the 0-24h/24h-7d model is practitioner heuristic, not empirical. Only Huang 2018 cat data exists as an analog. No dog-equivalent study published.

2. **No published breed-type outcome comparison study** — Albrecht 1999 (n=254, informal) is the only breed-distance data point. Lord 2007 doesn't break down by breed. No formal study exists.

3. **No dog-specific sighting reliability framework** — general eyewitness accuracy literature applies but no study of dog sighting reliability exists.

4. **No dog-specific Bayesian position update model** — SARBayes and Lin & Goodrich apply to humans. The ISRID dataset has no dog equivalent.

5. **Podenco-specific lost behavior data very sparse** — most sources cover podenco breed characteristics in general; no study of how podencos specifically behave when lost in Algarve terrain.

6. **Herding and guardian breed data almost absent** — territory-aware behavior is inferred from breed function; no lost-dog-specific studies found.
