summary: SNIRH (Sistema Nacional de Informação de Recursos Hídricos) is Portugal's
  national water resources information system, managed by APA (Agência Portuguesa
  do Ambiente), aggregating data from 1,500 monitoring stations and 10+ monitoring
  networks at time intervals from minutes to yearly. Contains hydrological time series
  data for Algarve rivers and dams. Access via snirh.apambiente.pt. Originally developed
  by Bluefocus until 2002 then transferred to INAG/APA. Three-tier Oracle architecture
  with alpha-numeric and geographic data. Key data source for seasonal flow, reservoir
  levels, and groundwater status across the Algarve.

Description
Design and development of the SNIRH until 2002, when it was transferred to INAG. SNIRH, now managed by the APA, brings together all data from more than 10 monitoring networks, 1500 stations and 250 associated parameters at time intervals ranging from minute to year. SNIRH has a three-tier architecture supported by an Oracle database that contains alpha-numeric and geographic data and allows querying the monitoring networks and the analysis of time series of environmental variables.
Objectives
To safeguard of monitoring data
To offer data analysis tools for water management
To make publicly available information on the monitoring of water resources
Results
A pioneering system that provides information on water resources since 1997 and that still remains operational today.
