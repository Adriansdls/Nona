What’s a “Galgo?”
The Galgo Español (aka the Spanish Galgo, or Spanish Greyhound) is a breed of sighthound currently facing a crisis in Spain. Used for hunting hares, these dogs are typically kept in abysmal conditions until they are disposed of in cruel and inhumane ways at the end of the hunting season, only for new dogs to be purchased each season from overpopulated breeding mills. The hunters that utilize Galgos, or “galgueros,” believe that the more their retired hunting dog suffers as it dies, the more successful their next hunting season will be.
Galgos share a number of similarities with their look-a-like, the English Greyhound. Athletic, intelligent, and sensitive, this historic breed deserves to be loved and cared for throughout its preservation.
